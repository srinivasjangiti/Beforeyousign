RESIDENTIAL LEASE AGREEMENT

This Lease Agreement ("Lease") is made as of January 1, 2025, between Property Management Solutions LLC ("Landlord") and the undersigned individual(s) ("Tenant").

PROPERTY: 123 Main Street, Unit 4B, Anytown, State 12345

1. TERM AND RENT
1.1 Initial term is twelve (12) months, automatically renewing for successive twelve-month periods unless Tenant provides one hundred eighty (180) days written notice before expiration.
1.2 Monthly rent is $2,500, due on the 1st of each month. Late fee of $200 applies immediately on the 2nd, plus $50 per day thereafter.
1.3 Rent increases by 15% upon each renewal, non-negotiable.
1.4 All payments must be by certified check or money order; personal checks not accepted.

2. SECURITY DEPOSIT
2.1 Security deposit of $7,500 (three months' rent) is required before move-in and is non-refundable.
2.2 Landlord may use deposit for any purpose and is not required to return any portion.
2.3 Normal wear and tear is defined as any deterioration whatsoever; Tenant is liable for all restoration costs.
2.4 Tenant must pay additional amounts if deposit is insufficient, within 5 days of demand.

3. MAINTENANCE AND REPAIRS
3.1 Tenant is responsible for ALL repairs and maintenance, including structural, mechanical, HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roof, foundation, and appliances.
3.2 Landlord has no duty to maintain the property in habitable condition.
3.3 Tenant must respond to maintenance requests within 2 hours, 24/7/365.
3.4 Landlord may enter property at any time without notice for any reason.
3.5 All repairs must be performed by contractors selected and charged by Landlord, with 50% markup.

4. UTILITIES AND SERVICES
4.1 Tenant pays all utilities including water, sewer, gas, electric, trash, internet, and cable.
4.2 Tenant pays $200 monthly "administrative fee" for Landlord's management services.
4.3 Tenant pays $150 monthly "amenity fee" regardless of whether amenities are used.
4.4 Fees are subject to change at any time without notice.

5. USE RESTRICTIONS
5.1 No guests may visit for more than 4 hours per month total without Landlord's written permission.
5.2 Tenant must be physically present in unit at least 300 days per year.
5.3 No pets, smoking, cooking with strong odors, playing musical instruments, or exercising in unit.
5.4 Quiet hours are 6:00 PM to 10:00 AM daily.
5.5 No storage of any items other than essential furniture and clothing.
5.6 Landlord monitors property via cameras in common areas and may install monitoring devices in unit.

6. ALTERATIONS AND IMPROVEMENTS
6.1 Any alterations, including hanging pictures, require written consent with $500 non-refundable processing fee per request.
6.2 All improvements become Landlord's property immediately upon installation.
6.3 Tenant must remove all improvements at Tenant's expense upon move-out and restore to original condition.

7. TERMINATION BY LANDLORD
7.1 Landlord may terminate this Lease at any time with 24 hours notice for any reason or no reason.
7.2 Upon termination, Tenant must vacate immediately and forfeit all rent paid for remaining term.
7.3 Landlord is not liable for any damages, relocation costs, or inconvenience.

8. TERMINATION BY TENANT
8.1 Tenant may only terminate by providing 180 days notice AND paying termination fee equal to 6 months' rent.
8.2 Early termination for any reason (job loss, military deployment, health issues, etc.) results in forfeiture of security deposit and liability for all rent through original term end date.
8.3 Tenant remains liable for rent until Landlord re-rents unit, at Landlord's discretion.

9. LANDLORD'S REMEDIES
9.1 Any violation of any term, regardless of severity, constitutes material breach.
9.2 Upon any breach, Landlord may: (a) terminate Lease immediately; (b) charge $1,000 penalty per violation; (c) evict Tenant; (d) change locks; (e) remove Tenant's belongings to storage at Tenant's expense.
9.3 Landlord may pursue all remedies simultaneously.
9.4 No cure period is provided for any violation.

10. LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION
10.1 Landlord is not liable for any injuries, damages, or losses occurring on or related to the property, regardless of Landlord's negligence.
10.2 Tenant assumes all risk of personal injury, property damage, theft, fire, flood, natural disasters, or other losses.
10.3 Tenant shall indemnify and defend Landlord from all claims by Tenant, guests, contractors, or third parties.
10.4 Tenant must maintain renter's insurance with $1,000,000 liability coverage naming Landlord as additional insured and primary beneficiary.

11. DISPUTE RESOLUTION
11.1 Any dispute shall be resolved exclusively through binding arbitration in Landlord's choice of venue.
11.2 Tenant waives all rights to jury trial or court proceedings.
11.3 Tenant shall pay all arbitration costs, Landlord's attorney fees, and $500/hour for Landlord's time.
11.4 Arbitration is confidential; Tenant may not disclose existence or results to anyone.
11.5 If Tenant violates arbitration provision, Tenant pays $50,000 penalty plus Landlord's damages.

12. DEFAULT AND ACCELERATION
12.1 Default occurs if Tenant: (a) is late on rent; (b) violates any term; (c) files bankruptcy; (d) has financial difficulties; or (e) for any other reason Landlord determines.
12.2 Upon default, entire rent for remaining term becomes immediately due in full.
12.3 Landlord may report default to credit bureaus and rental history databases.

13. RULES AND REGULATIONS
13.1 Tenant shall comply with all building rules as amended by Landlord from time to time.
13.2 Rules may be changed without notice. Violation of any rule is a material breach.
13.3 Landlord's interpretation of rules is final and binding.

14. SUBORDINATION
This Lease is subordinate to any mortgages. Tenant agrees to attorn to any purchaser or lender and waives all rights in foreclosure.

15. NO WARRANTIES
15.1 Property is leased "AS IS" without any warranties.
15.2 Landlord makes no representations about condition, habitability, safety, or fitness for any purpose.
15.3 Tenant has inspected property and accepts all defects, known or unknown.

16. ASSIGNMENT AND SUBLETTING
16.1 Tenant may not assign or sublet under any circumstances.
16.2 Any attempt to assign or sublet results in immediate termination and $10,000 penalty.
16.3 If Tenant dies or is incapacitated, estate remains liable for all obligations through term.

17. ATTORNEY FEES
If any dispute arises, Tenant shall pay Landlord's attorney fees and costs, regardless of outcome, within 15 days of demand.

18. NOTICES
Notices to Tenant may be by email, text, posting on door, or verbal communication. Notices to Landlord must be by certified mail to Landlord's attorney with 30 days advance delivery.

19. SEVERABILITY
If any provision is found unenforceable, it shall be modified to be enforceable to maximum extent, and remaining provisions remain in effect.

20. ENTIRE AGREEMENT
This Lease is the entire agreement. Any oral statements are not binding. Only written amendments signed by Landlord are valid.

21. WAIVER
Landlord's failure to enforce any provision does not waive any rights. Tenant's failure to comply results in immediate waiver of all Tenant rights.

22. GOVERNING LAW
This Lease is governed by [Landlord's preferred state] law. Exclusive venue is [Landlord's county] courts.

23. ACKNOWLEDGMENT
Tenant acknowledges having read and understood all terms, having had opportunity to consult legal counsel (whether or not Tenant did so), and voluntarily agrees to be bound.
